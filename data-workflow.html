What happens with your data?

You do not need to upload raw videos. Instead, you upload the resulting 3D model (mesh / textured model) to a shared space.

Models are stored in an open repository (initially cloud storage, then long-term archiving on open platforms such as Zenodo) under a Creative Commons license so they can be reused for research, conservation and AI development.

In short:
  • You keep control of your original footage.
  • The shared 3D models are open for non-commercial research and conservation.
  • Metadata is kept as simple as possible but scientifically useful.

Suggested workflow for model capture and upload

  1. Record a clean video loop around the coral.
  2. Load the video into a 3D reconstruction app (e.g. KIRI Engine or Luma).
  3. Let the app generate the 3D model.
  4. Export the model in a standard format (e.g. GLB, OBJ, or similar).
  5. Fill in the participant sheet with metadata.
  6. Upload the model + metadata to the shared drive (link provided by the project).
