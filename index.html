Mission

Adopt-a-Coral is a crowd-science movement aiming to capture corals over time, one coral at a time.

Our mission is to:

  • Provide a voice for corals in the Caribbean and show the world how they change in real time via crowd-science.
  • Democratize 3D imaging technology for coral reef ecology.
  • Develop a community-based dataset to enable the next generation of AI tools for coral research and management.
Why the Caribbean?

Caribbean reefs are the lifeline of coastal communities, providing food, coastal protection, tourism income and cultural identity. At the same time, they are among the fastest-declining reefs on the planet.

Reef-building stony corals are turning from living structures into rocks covered in algae. Losing them means losing biodiversity and the services reefs provide.

Caribbean reefs need us the most.

The gaps we are trying to fill

1. Time gap in monitoring

Most marine scientists spend a few months per year in the field and the rest in the lab. That is not enough to document changes as they are happening on the reef.

Our solution: work with local dive shops and NGOs that dive the reef every day to document changes in individual coral colonies.

2. AI not fully used for nature conservation

AI technologies have enormous potential for detecting, classifying and tracking changes in coral reefs, but the training datasets for colony-scale change are extremely limited.

Our solution: build an open, long-term 3D dataset of corals that can be used to train AI models for reef management.

Get involved

You don’t need to be a scientist to help. If you dive or snorkel, you can adopt a coral and start contributing to this dataset.
